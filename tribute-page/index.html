Nguyễn Du 阮攸

One of greatest poets in Vietnam

Here's a time line of Mr. Nguyen Du's life

  • 1975: Born in Bich Cau, Thang Long, Hanoi
  • 1775: Nguyễn lost his father, he also lost his mother at age 13, so for most of his teen years he lived with his brother Nguyễn Khản or with his brother-in-law Đoàn Nguyễn Tuấn.
  • 1784: At the age of 19 (some sources say 17), Nguyễn passed the provincial examination and received the title of "tú tài" (Bachelor's degree), which made him (very roughly) the equivalent of a high school graduate.
  • 1813: While in China, he discovered and translated the Ming dynasty era tale that would become the basis for the Tale of Kieu
  • 1820: Published the Tale of Kieu
  • He was later appointed to two more diplomatic missions to Peking, but before he could depart for the last one, he died of a long illness for which he refused treatment.

The Tale of Kiều (Vietnamese Truyện Kiều) was based on an earlier Chinese prose narrative, Kim Vân Kiều. It was written under a pen-name as the story was quite critical of the basic tenets of Confucian morality. It is a tragic tale of two lovers forced apart by the girl's loyalty to her family honor. In Vietnam, the poem is so popular and beloved that some people know the whole epic by heart and can recite it without a mistake
